# CHRISTIAN HIP-HOP GRAPHIC DESIGN PLAYBOOK
> The formula. Studied from Cey Adams, Eric Haze, Sk8thing, NIGO, KAWS, CPFM, Verdy, Brain Dead, Heron Preston, Awake NY. Seasoned with Christian content. Designed to span Whisper → Speak → Statement → Sermon → Shout.

## The thesis (one paragraph)
FTC's quiet luxury lane (Lemaire / The Row / ALD restraint) is the brand's center. But the graphic tee is a parallel lane with its own rules — bold, expressive, generous. The current Christian streetwear graphic tee market (Reach Records merch + Etsy "John 3:16" tees) is craft-weak. The opportunity: design Christian graphic tees at the design-grade of CPFM, with the layered-reference discipline of Brain Dead, the community-rooted specificity of Awake NY, and the Cey Adams 3-color rule throughout — without ever directly citing verses, using crosses-as-decoration, or operating in church-camp typography. The Sermon-tier graphic tee should be wearable at a CPFM-Drake show OR a Hillsong service without standing out as wrong in either.

## The 10 commandments (Christian hip-hop graphic tee edition)

### 1. NEVER cite verses directly
"John 3:16" on the front = the Etsy-tier trap. Acceptable: a fragment of the verse stripped of citation. "GOD SO LOVED" stripped of context. Customer who knows, knows.

### 2. NEVER use the cross as decoration
Section 1 forbidden term. Already locked. Reinforce here.

### 3. Max 3 colors per tee
Cey Adams discipline. One color MUST be black (`#15110D`) or bone (`#EFE9D8`).

### 4. Type does the work
The graphic tee carries text more than image. Type treatment is the design. Image is secondary.

### 5. Hand-drawn naive type for Sermon-tier
CPFM principle. Wonky letterforms as luxury signal. Commission one designer's hand; lock it.

### 6. One motif per capsule for Statement-tier
Verdy / KAWS principle. A whole capsule on one drawing.

### 7. Layered references for Sermon-tier
Brain Dead principle. Stack: theological text + tonal pattern + historical-reference fragment.

### 8. Christian content via fragment
Augustine quotes broken into 2-3 word fragments. Hymn lyrics treated as poetry. Greek (New Testament) / Hebrew / Latin (Vulgate) used decoratively. Customers Google. The hunt is part of the relationship.

### 9. Restraint palette even when bold
Sermon-tier graphic in CPFM-grade design with FTC's earth-neutral 5-tone palette. Sweet visual + serious content + muted color = the FTC bold signal.

### 10. Sincerity always
Off-White wink? No. CPFM playful? Yes — but sincerely. The faith is felt. Never ironic. Never bootleg.

## Drop architecture

Every drop should ship:
- **3-5 Tier 1 pieces** (Whisper — main FTC restraint line)
- **2-3 Tier 2 pieces** (Speak — subtle graphics)
- **2-3 Tier 3 pieces** (Statement — single bold motif)
- **1-2 Tier 4 pieces** (Sermon — loud graphic tees)
- **0-1 Tier 5 pieces** (Shout — only special capsules)

Total: 8-14 pieces per drop. Tier spread creates the BRAND. A customer can pick their own volume.

## Common failure modes (Christian hip-hop graphic tee specific)

### The Etsy trap
Direct verse citation + crucifix imagery + Comic Sans font + low-GSM blank.
Sign: looks like a youth pastor designed it Tuesday night.
Fix: every commandment above.

### The performative trap
Graphics that signal "I'm Christian" loudly to non-Christians.
Sign: the wearer is performing rather than belonging.
Fix: Christian content carries the WEARER inward, not the brand outward.

### The bootleg trap
Christian-version of a secular brand (Cocaine-of-Christ, Calvin Kleen-ses Us).
Sign: cringe immediate.
Fix: original work only.

### The over-reference trap
Stacking too many obscure references until customers can't decode anything.
Sign: design reads cluttered.
Fix: 2-3 references max per tee. Brain Dead at his best, not his worst.

### The youth-pastor-aesthetic trap
Graffiti font + checkered pattern + ambiguous spirituality.
Sign: looks like a $20 Hot Topic Christian shirt with the price-tag still on.
Fix: commit to design grade. Commission real designers. Pay craft prices.
