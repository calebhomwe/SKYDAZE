# NIKE — brand study

> The mother. $51B revenue (FY2024). Defined the modern athletic-streetwear hybrid. Built the playbook every brand on this list reads.

## Founding logic
- Premise: athletes are the gods of the modern city. Worship is performance gear.
- Aesthetic ancestor: Adidas (the rival), Onitsuka Tiger (the OG distributor), Russell Athletic (collegiate).
- Customer: everyone. Genius scale move — Nike makes products for runners, basketball players, ASAP Rocky, and 8-year-old Tuesday-night soccer kids.

## Visual codes
| Element | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| Color palette | Full spectrum — but per-line palette discipline. Air Max line: 80s primaries. ACG: utility orange/olive. NikeLab: monochrome. |
| Logos | The Swoosh + "NIKE" or just the Swoosh. Placement varies wildly by product (chest, hem, sleeve, sole). |
| Typography | Futura Bold (historical), Trade Gothic (current). Always uppercase. |
| Silhouettes | Sport-functional first, fashion second. Air Force 1, Air Max 90, Cortez, Dunk, Blazer — each a category-defining silhouette. |
| Materials | Engineered mesh, Flyknit, Vaporweave, leather, ripstop nylon, Dri-FIT. |
| Footwear | The brand IS footwear. Every other category exists to support sneaker sales. |

## Strategic moves
- **Sub-brand architecture.** Nike SB, NikeLab, ACG, Jordan, Nike Pro — each has its own visual language while sharing the Swoosh.
- **Storytelling at scale.** Every ad is a story (Bo Jackson, Tiger Woods, Serena, LeBron). Product appears almost incidentally.
- **Collab as cultural currency.** Off-White × Nike (Ten), Travis Scott × Nike, sacai × Nike, Patta × Nike. Each one bridges Nike to a culture Nike doesn't natively own.
- **Retro/heritage cycling.** Re-release archive silhouettes (Dunk, AF1) every 7-10 years to capture new generations. The well never runs dry.
- **DTC dominance.** SNKRS app + Nike.com drove DTC from 16% (2010) to 44% (2024) of revenue. Margin compounding.

## What we steal
1. **Sub-brand architecture.** FTC could have: FTC Heritage (drops), FTC Sanctum (limited religious/liturgical pieces), FTC Movement (athletic-leaning, for the campus crowd). One spirit, three product lines.
2. **Heritage cycling.** Treat FTC's first drops as archive. Re-release "Cornerstone 001" in a refined material 5 years later. Tells the customer the brand is permanent.
3. **App-as-channel.** SNKRS is Nike's most loyal customer touchpoint. **This is exactly the iPhone app we're building for FTC.**
4. **Storytelling over product hero.** The product is almost incidental. The story is the hero.
5. **Collab to bridge cultures.** FTC × a gospel-rap artist (Lecrae?), FTC × a Black church (specific congregation collab), FTC × a Christian college (Wheaton? Baylor?) — bridges, not extensions.

## What we avoid
1. **Scale dilution.** Nike's mass-market identity makes premium positioning hard. FTC must stay limited.
2. **Sport-first identity.** FTC is not a performance brand. Athletic-influenced, not athletic.
3. **Logo dominance.** The Swoosh works at Nike's scale. At FTC's scale, restraint reads louder than a logo.

## Typography lessons
- **One typeface per sub-line.** Air Max uses Futura. ACG uses condensed sans. NikeLab uses Helvetica Neue Thin. FTC needs typography by sub-line.
- **Uppercase always for headers.** It's a uniform. Lowercase reads consumer-friendly; uppercase reads premium.
- **Tracking discipline.** Nike's titles are letter-spaced at 5–7%. FTC should adopt 4–6% for all product titles.

## Photography & art direction
- **Athlete portraits, mid-effort.** Nike shoots athletes at the moment of physical truth — sweat, tension, breath. FTC's lifestyle should aim for the same emotional honesty (without the sport context).
- **Editorial b-roll over still product.** Nike's product imagery feels secondary to the story. FTC has been over-investing in flat product shots — shift 30% to editorial.
- **Slow-motion as signature.** Phantom flex cameras at 1000+ fps. FTC's video pod should adopt the same — slow fabric drape over fast-cut commerce.

## Materials worth replicating
- **Engineered mesh** for accessories (the cap brim, the bag panel).
- **Reflective 3M tape** for outerwear accents (subtle — single seam line, not panels).
- **Heavy-knit ribbing** at cuffs/hems for that 90s warmup-jacket weight.

## SNKRS app — the model for FTC's iPhone app
SNKRS is the gold standard. Features to replicate:
1. **Push notifications for drops** — not email, push.
2. **Stored payment + size** — one-tap checkout under scarcity pressure.
3. **Stories tab** — every product gets a 90-second story before you can buy.
4. **Member-only early access** — tier-based via account history.
5. **Local store integration** — "available at your nearest store" reduces shipping anxiety.

FTC iPhone app must have at minimum:
1. Drop notifications.
2. Generative concept preview (this is OUR differentiator — Nike doesn't have this).
3. Story-first product view.
4. SMS-tier access.
5. Lookbook gallery.

## ACG (All Conditions Gear) — the closest aesthetic cousin
ACG is the Nike sub-line FTC should study most carefully:
- Utility-forward but cinematic
- Olive/earth/charcoal palette
- Sparse typography ("ACG" wordmark only)
- Modular outerwear (zip-off, pack-down)
- Marketed via single still images, never crowds

FTC's outerwear category should look like ACG with the Swoosh replaced by silence.

## Failure modes to avoid
- **Over-collab dilution.** Late-2010s Nike was running 3+ major collabs per quarter. Each one cannibalizes the next.
- **DTC under-investment in legacy retailers.** Nike pulled out of Foot Locker/Macy's too fast (2020–2022), bled wholesale revenue, partially reversed (2024). Lesson: DTC growth shouldn't be at the expense of wholesale relationships overnight.
- **Innovation theater.** Air technology stopped genuinely innovating around 2015. New names (Tuned Air, Joyride) felt like rebrands of Air. Product must change, not just naming.

## TL;DR — apply to FTC
1. **Sub-brand architecture** — Heritage / Sanctum / Movement.
2. **Heritage cycling** — re-release earliest drops as archive at year 5.
3. **iPhone app as primary customer touchpoint** — modeled on SNKRS, differentiated by generative preview.
4. **Athlete-honest lifestyle imagery** — even without sport.
5. **One collab/year that bridges cultures FTC doesn't own** — gospel-rap artist, Christian college, Black church congregation.
