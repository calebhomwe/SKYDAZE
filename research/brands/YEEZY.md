# YEEZY — brand study

> Kanye West × adidas Originals (2015–2022), then Yeezy independent. Defined "monastic minimal streetwear" for the 2010s and forced the industry to take Ye seriously as a designer.

## Founding logic
- Premise: streetwear should feel like uniform, not costume.
- Aesthetic ancestor: Helmut Lang minimalism + Rick Owens proportions + military surplus utility.
- Customer: 18–34 male who reads i-D, follows architecture pages, and listens to Travis Scott.

## Visual codes
| Element | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| Color palette | Sand, bone, stone, charcoal, oxidized rust, ash. No primaries. Saturation < 0.35. |
| Logos | Mostly absent. When present: small woven label, interior tag, blind embossing. |
| Typography | None visible on garments. Branding via silhouette + materials. |
| Silhouettes | Oversized, dropped shoulder, elongated torso, gusseted crotch, cropped hem, wide leg with taper. |
| Materials | 320–400 gsm fleece, garment-dyed cotton, military nylon, calfskin, mohair, Mongolian fur (Season 1). |
| Footwear | Boost foam, Primeknit upper, hidden lacing, monochrome colorways, foam runner sculpture (2020+). |

## Strategic moves
- **Drop scarcity.** Limited runs, no restocks. Manufactured exclusivity even when production capacity allowed more.
- **Provocation marketing.** Each season-launch campaign was visually disorienting (Season 1: refugee aesthetic; Season 4: Roosevelt Island in heat).
- **Music as brand vehicle.** Album rollouts (Pablo, Donda) doubled as fashion campaigns.
- **Athletes as models.** Football players over runway models. Communicated "uniform for movement," not "uniform for posing."
- **Gallery shows over runway.** Avoided fashion-week calendar entirely after Season 4.

## What we steal
1. **Silhouette obsession.** Spend the design budget on proportion before any print.
2. **Logo restraint.** Identity through shape and material, not text.
3. **Palette discipline.** Lock a 5-tone earth-neutral system per drop and refuse to break it.
4. **Footwear-as-sculpture.** If FTC ever does footwear, treat it as object first, shoe second.
5. **Drop scarcity discipline.** One run. No restocks. Defend the brand by saying no.

## What we avoid
1. **Public-figure volatility.** Tying brand value to one person's mood is a fragility tax. FTC's center is theology, not a personality.
2. **Refugee/exploitation imagery.** Some Yeezy campaigns crossed lines that FTC's Christian ethic explicitly forbids.
3. **Pricing without warranty.** $200 t-shirts that pill in 3 washes destroys trust. FTC garments must outlast the customer's first child.

## Typography lessons
- When type appears: micro-scale (8–10pt), tonal, interior tag only.
- Sans grotesque only — Helvetica Neue Light, Akzidenz, Founders Grotesk.
- Custom letterforms drawn for Yeezy season identity (Season 5 onward). FTC should commission custom letterforms before drop 3.

## Photography & art direction
- Available light only. North-window or overcast outdoor.
- Wide lens, model centered, three-quarter or back-of-head crop.
- Backgrounds: concrete, plaster, painted backdrop in a tonal mid-grey.
- Editing: zero saturation lift. Slight lift in shadows for film feel. No skin smoothing.

## Materials worth replicating
- Bonded fleece (300–320 gsm) with garment-dye.
- Recycled denim canvas for outerwear.
- Recycled rubber sole compounds (V2-era boost direction).
- Tonal woven labels — 40-thread, color-matched to garment shade.

## Failure modes to avoid
- "Yeezy fatigue" by Season 6 came from over-extending the same silhouette without iterating. FTC must evolve silhouette family every 3 drops.
- Quality regressions in late-era Yeezy (post-2021) drove customers to Fear of God Essentials. Margin discipline must never erode physical product quality.

## TL;DR — apply to FTC
1. **No logos on the front.** Identity is silhouette + material.
2. **Sand + bone + oxidized rust + ash + onyx.** Five-tone earth palette per drop.
3. **One-run scarcity.** Defend the brand by saying no.
4. **Athlete-cast lifestyle imagery.** Movement over posing.
5. **Stop short of provocation.** Restraint is the brand's deepest moat.
