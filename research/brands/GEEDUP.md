# GEEDUP — brand study

> Australian streetwear. Sydney-founded ~2003, hit international consciousness 2018-2024. Genuine outsider streetwear — built in Western Sydney, not Manhattan or LA. Heavily influenced by Aussie rap (Hilltop Hoods, ChillinIT, OneFour) and Polynesian / Pasifika culture.

## Why this matters for FTC
- Geedup is the closest analog to what FTC could be: a faith/community-rooted streetwear brand from a non-traditional design city, that's broken globally.
- Perth, Sydney, Brisbane → diaspora networks → London → US.
- Geedup proves you don't need NYC/LA infrastructure to build a serious streetwear brand.

## Founding logic
- Premise: Aussie / Pacific streetwear is its own thing. Don't import American hypebeast aesthetics — codify what Western Sydney already wears.
- Aesthetic ancestor: Stone Island (utility outerwear), Stüssy (early bootleg-tee energy), Dickies (utility workwear), early Supreme.
- Customer: 18–28, Australia + UK + NZ + Pacific Islander diaspora globally. Now also picked up by US heads.

## Visual codes
| Element | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| Color palette | Dusty earth, sun-faded navy, faded olive, washed-out red. Designed to look like a 2-year-old garment from day one. |
| Logos | Bold serif wordmark "GEEDUP." Often arched/curved. Sometimes Old English (a controversial choice — see "What we avoid"). |
| Typography | Bold serifs (Trajan-like), arched layouts, occasionally Old English / Blackletter (we'd skip this). |
| Silhouettes | Boxy heavyweight tees, panel hoodies, cargo work shorts, sun-faded outerwear. |
| Materials | 250-320gsm heavyweight cotton, washed canvas, ripstop. |
| Footwear | Doesn't really do footwear — collaborates with New Balance occasionally. |

## Strategic moves
- **Drop schedule is THE marketing.** Geedup drops 4-6 times per year. Each one sells out in minutes. The waitlist is the customer list.
- **Local first, global second.** Western Sydney specific — references to suburbs (Mt Druitt, Bankstown), local rappers (OneFour, ChillinIT), Pacific cultural elements. Geedup doesn't try to be "international" — it IS Western Sydney, and that became its global appeal.
- **Authenticity through specificity.** Geedup graphics reference specific cricket teams, specific Pacific Islander cultural moments, specific Aussie slang. Outsiders feel like they're being let in on something real.
- **Restraint on social.** Geedup's Instagram is sparse compared to streetwear peers. Less is more.
- **Drops as events.** In-store launch days in Sydney. Hundreds of people queue. The brand IS its community.

## What we steal
1. **Hyper-local specificity.** FTC should be aggressively diaspora-specific. Reference DMV go-go culture by name (Chuck Brown, "Backyard Band"). Reference Scarborough TTC routes by number ("the 86"). Reference Maryland's Old Bay, Baltimore club music's specific BPM (140-149). Specificity = authenticity.
2. **Drop-as-event.** Even online drops should have a moment of community. In-app launch countdowns, livestream Q&As with the founder, locked-content "you were here when" badges.
3. **Local rapper collaborations.** Geedup × OneFour, ChillinIT. FTC × gospel-rap artists from each diaspora city. Lecrae (Atlanta), Hulvey (Atlanta), Limoblaze (Nigeria/UK), KB (Houston), nobigdyl (Nashville). One per drop, max one per year per artist.
4. **Sun-faded / pre-aged garment treatment.** Geedup garments look 18 months old on day one. FTC could borrow this for one product line ("Heritage Wash") — pre-faded denim and tees that feel inherited.

## What we avoid
1. **Old English / blackletter typography.** Carries gang/cholo aesthetic baggage in some contexts; carries supremacist baggage in others. FTC's Christian foundation cannot risk this connotation. Use serif (Trajan, Caslon, Genath) but never blackletter.
2. **Cricket/rugby references that don't translate.** Hyper-local works only when the locality is yours. Don't fake it.
3. **Drug references.** Geedup occasionally crosses into territory that doesn't fit FTC's ethic.
4. **All-male brand identity.** Geedup is very masculine-coded. FTC womenswear should equal-weight.

## Typography lessons
- **Bold serif as identity.** Geedup's wordmark is bold serif on every piece. FTC's wordmark direction could be similar — custom serif drawn for FTC.
- **Arched type.** Geedup curves their wordmark over chest placements. FTC could borrow this for select pieces (caps, hoodies). Risk: reads collegiate. Use sparingly.

## Photography & art direction
- Lifestyle imagery shot in suburban Sydney specifically — Westfields, train stations, suburb backyards.
- Models are friends, local rappers, community members. Never agency.
- Lighting: harsh Aussie sun, no fill. Embraces the "I shot this on a Sunday at 3pm" feel.
- Color grade: slightly faded, like an old film roll.

FTC translation:
- Lifestyle shot in diaspora cities — Brooklyn brownstones, DMV metro stations, Scarborough TTC routes, Peckham high streets.
- Models from those communities. Never agency.
- Available light, embrace honest light conditions.
- Subtle film grain in post; not over-corrected.

## Materials worth replicating
- **250–320gsm pigment-dyed cotton.** Geedup's signature feel — heavy but soft, with fade built in.
- **Washed canvas outerwear.** For FTC's outerwear category, study Geedup's "work jacket" cut.
- **Embroidered patches** on caps and pockets. Tonal embroidery aligns perfectly with FTC's restraint.

## Failure modes to learn from
- **Old English controversy.** Geedup has been called out for typography that reads as gang-adjacent. FTC must not.
- **Limited womenswear.** Brand identity has been mostly menswear; the female customer is significant but underserved. FTC should not repeat this.
- **Bot/reseller pressure.** Drop scarcity creates resale markets. Geedup hasn't solved this. FTC's iPhone app should implement bot-detection (device fingerprinting, behavioral signals).

## TL;DR — apply to FTC
1. **Hyper-local specificity.** Reference real diaspora cities by name, route, neighborhood, slang.
2. **Drop-as-event.** Build community moments around every drop via the iPhone app.
3. **One local artist collab per drop.** Gospel-rap-aligned, not generic.
4. **Pre-aged "Heritage Wash" product line** for an inherited-feel sub-line.
5. **Avoid blackletter typography** at all costs.
6. **Bot-detection in iPhone app** from day one — don't let resellers eat the customer's experience.
