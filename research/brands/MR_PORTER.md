# MR PORTER — brand & merchandising study

> Net-a-Porter Group's menswear platform, launched 2011. Reached $1.1B GMV by 2018. Defined "luxury menswear ecommerce." Sold to Farfetch in 2020 (now under Coupang). The reference point for how to merchandise luxury menswear online.

## Why this matters for FTC
- Mr Porter is the gold standard for digital luxury merchandising — product page layout, editorial integration, brand-story-driven discovery.
- FTC sells direct-to-consumer at luxury prices through both Shopify and the iPhone app. The merchandising patterns Mr Porter perfected should be adopted, not invented.
- The Journal (Mr Porter's editorial wing) is one of the few brand magazines that genuinely sells product. Worth deep study.

## Founding logic
- Premise: men want luxury menswear delivered with the same editorial confidence as Net-a-Porter delivers womenswear.
- Aesthetic ancestor: Esquire / GQ / The Rake / Drake's catalog culture.
- Customer: 30-55 male, $200k+ household income, urban, design-aware, comfortable spending $400-2000 per garment.

## The 5-section product page (lifted from Mr Porter)

This is the most important pattern in the brand study. Mr Porter pioneered this structure; every luxury menswear ecom site since has copied it:

1. **Hero gallery** — 6-12 images. Product on white + product on model + product detail macros.
2. **Editorial paragraph** — 100-300 words. NOT marketing copy. Real writing about the maker, the heritage, the construction. Often signed by an editor.
3. **Spec block** — material (with country/mill), measurements, fit notes, care instructions, country of origin.
4. **"Shop the look"** — 3-5 complementary pieces styled with the hero garment. Cross-sell engine.
5. **"From the journal"** — links to long-form editorial features that include this product.

FTC PDPs must match this 5-section structure exactly. The brand-research playbook (`STREETWEAR_PLAYBOOK.md`) already includes this. Reinforcing.

## Strategic moves
- **The Journal as primary marketing.** Long-form editorial, deeply produced, weekly cadence. Real journalism — interviews with designers, makers, athletes. Drives 40%+ of new-customer acquisition.
- **The Style Council.** 8-12 "best dressed" Mr Porter customers globally, profiled annually. Customers want to BE these people. Pure aspiration content.
- **Editor's Picks.** Site navigation organized partly by editor (Wei Koh, Jeremy Langmead). Customers learn editor taste, follow editor recommendations.
- **The Daily.** Daily email + push notification of "what's new." Not "sale ends today" — actual curation.
- **Free returns + free delivery over $200.** Removed every purchase-anxiety friction.
- **Authenticity guarantee.** Every product certified authentic by Mr Porter. Critical in a category with grey-market resale.
- **Mr Porter Health In Mind initiative.** Brand stand on men's mental health. Not adjacent to fashion at all — and yet exactly the right move for the demo.

## What FTC steals
1. **The 5-section product page exactly** — already in the playbook, reinforce in Shopify build.
2. **The Journal as editorial backbone** — FTC's Substack should mature toward Journal-quality long-form.
3. **Editor's Picks navigation** — FTC's Shopify could surface "The Steward's Picks" (founder curation) and "From The Vestry" (community curation) as primary nav.
4. **Daily curation email/push** — not sales, real curation. "Today: how the cornerstone tee fits over a 320gsm hoodie."
5. **Free returns + free delivery threshold** — luxury customer expects this. Bake into operations.
6. **Brand stand outside fashion** — FTC's faith foundation IS the equivalent of Mr Porter's Health In Mind. Lean into it through editorial coverage of mental health, faith, community work.

## What FTC avoids
1. **Multi-brand carrier model.** Mr Porter carries 300+ brands. FTC is one brand. Different merchandising logic — but the product-page pattern still applies.
2. **Mass-affluent positioning.** Mr Porter targets the upper end of luxury menswear ($400-2000). FTC sits one tier below ($80-480) — accessibly luxury, not aspirationally luxury. Adjust voice accordingly.
3. **Style Council as influencer program.** Mr Porter's profile-the-customer move only works at their scale. FTC's equivalent is the 12 Anchor creators — different mechanic, similar outcome.

## Editorial benchmarks — The Journal
The Journal publishes ~10 long-form pieces per week. Categories worth studying for FTC's Substack:
- "The Look" — single fit, single garment, deep explanation of the styling decision.
- "The Maker" — 2000-word interview with a designer / craftsperson / mill.
- "The Reference" — 1500-word essay on a cultural or historical reference (a film, a city, a movement).
- "Health In Mind" — first-person essays on men's mental health.
- "Five Things" — short curated list (5 books, 5 albums, 5 destinations).

FTC's Substack equivalents:
- "The Garment" — single piece, deep construction + theology rationale.
- "The Steward" — long interview with a maker / theologian / craftsperson.
- "The Reference" — essay on a place, a hymn, a building, a season.
- "Faith Without Cringe" — first-person essays on lived faith and design culture.
- "Five Things" — same format, FTC voice.

## Photography & art direction
- White cyclorama product shots: dead-center, dead-flat, identical across catalog.
- Editorial: real environments, often with model walking through a city or interior.
- Color grading: very flat, almost no color shift in post.
- Slight underexposure for editorial mood.
- Never crop tightly; generous breathing room.

## Material transparency standard
Every Mr Porter PDP includes:
- Fabric composition (e.g., "100% Italian wool, sourced from Drago mill in Biella")
- GSM (where applicable)
- Country of origin
- Sustainability certifications (where applicable)

FTC must match this. The brand-side playbook already specifies it. Mr Porter is the proof it works.

## Failure modes Mr Porter has avoided
- Never discounted heavily. End-of-season sales exist but at modest depth.
- Never let UX speed drift. Site loads under 1.5 seconds on 4G consistently.
- Never let the editorial drift toward sponsored content. The Journal stays editorially independent.

## Failure modes to learn from
- **Over-reliance on the carrier model.** Mr Porter is dependent on brands wanting to sell through Mr Porter. As Bottega Veneta, Loro Piana, Loewe expand DTC, Mr Porter's leverage erodes. Lesson: control your own distribution.
- **YOOX Net-a-Porter integration drag.** The 2015 YOOX merger and 2018 Richemont acquisition created governance complexity that slowed product velocity. Lesson: don't let M&A activity slow product.
- **Post-Coupang transition uncertainty.** Brand identity has wobbled slightly since the 2023 Coupang acquisition. Lesson: founders matter.
