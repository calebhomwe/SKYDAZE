# BOOHOOMAN — brand study (cautionary)

> UK fast-fashion menswear. Owned by Boohoo Group plc. The opposite of FTC in almost every way — but FTC must understand BoohooMAN deeply to know what NOT to do, AND to learn from their go-to-market efficiency.

## Why study a brand we'd never be
1. **Ad production pacing.** BoohooMAN ships 200+ new SKUs per week and produces ad creative weekly. The volume + speed of their creative production is something serious brands can learn from operationally — without copying the product ethic.
2. **Mobile-first commerce.** BoohooMAN's mobile experience is best-in-class for fast fashion. Speed of checkout, search, filter, image loading. The iPhone app we're building should match this UX speed (with completely different product ethics).
3. **Cinematic ad style.** Despite the cheap product, BoohooMAN's video ads have a specific cinematic look — handheld, slightly grainy, urban backdrops, fast-cut — that FTC's cinematic ad pod (Tier 15) directly references for pacing while inverting for taste.

## Founding logic (read with criticism)
- Premise: men want what women's fast fashion gave women — high volume, low price, trend-cycle responsiveness.
- Aesthetic ancestor: ASOS, Pretty Little Thing (sister brand), Misguided.
- Customer: 16–24, UK + Australia + US, $20–40 garment price tolerance.

## Visual codes
| Element | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| Color palette | Whatever's trending — primaries, neons in summer, earth tones when "quiet luxury" goes mainstream. Reactive, not authored. |

## Operational excellence (the parts to steal)

### Ad production speed
- BoohooMAN produces 40-60 short-form video ads per week.
- Single-shot model walks, basic cyclorama or studio, 12-second clips.
- Edited in same day. Live on social within 36 hours.
- **FTC lesson:** the cinematic ad pipeline must be capable of generating new variants in <48 hours. Our generative pipeline (DeepSeek Flash + Visual Qwen + Novita video) should target this cycle time.

### Pricing display
- "Was £29.99 / Now £14.99" framing on every product.
- 60% off is the default discount level — so customers learn that 60% off is "normal price."
- **FTC lesson (inverted):** never use crossed-out pricing. Never run "% off" sales. FTC garments are valued by the work, not the discount.

### Mobile UX
- Sub-second product image loading.
- Infinite scroll with category bar always visible.
- One-tap add-to-basket.
- Stored payment + size = checkout in 2 taps.
- **FTC lesson:** match the speed, invert the ethic. Same speed, different feel.

### Customer service
- 24/7 WhatsApp support.
- 365-day returns.
- **FTC lesson:** worth matching the responsiveness. Differentiate by quality of human contact — not bots, real humans who care about garments.

## What we MUST avoid

### Product
- Polyester-heavy garment construction.
- Sub-200gsm fabric weight.
- Pre-treated "fashion" finishes (acid wash, distressing) that fail in 2 washes.
- No country-of-origin transparency.
- No fabric specs on product pages.

### Marketing
- Crossed-out pricing.
- "Limited time only — 60% off!" urgency tactics.
- Influencer-of-the-week disposable casting.
- Trend-chasing without authorial point of view.
- "Fast fashion" voice in copy — exclamation points, "shop now!", emoji-heavy.

### Sustainability
- BoohooMAN was named one of the worst fast-fashion polluters by Greenpeace (2019, 2022).
- FTC must over-disclose sustainability — fabric origins, dyeing process, labor conditions, recycled content percentage.

## Visual elements we can learn from (without copying)

### Cinematic ad style (Tier 15 references this)
- Handheld camera, slight motion blur.
- Urban backdrops (London council estates, multi-story carparks, train station entrances).
- Fast-cut: 0.8-1.2 seconds per shot.
- Audio: drill or UK rap, low-bass-heavy.
- Sub-bass at -6dB to create the "felt not heard" sensation.

**FTC inversion:**
- Same handheld energy, but at half-speed.
- Same urban backdrops, but lit at dawn or dusk (not high noon).
- Slower cuts: 1.5-2.5 seconds per shot.
- Audio: same low-bass weight, but layered with church organ undertones or strings.

### Photography for product
- Cyclorama, top-down softbox, even fill.
- Model centered, dead expression.
- 3 shots per product: front, back, detail.

**FTC inversion:**
- Available light, north window or overcast.
- Model off-center or partially out-of-frame.
- 8+ shots per product including hand-on-fabric tactile shots.

## Pricing positioning chart (FTC vs BoohooMAN)
| Item | BoohooMAN price | FTC price | Ratio |
| :--- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Basic tee | £10-15 | £80-120 | 6-10x |
| Heavyweight hoodie | £25-35 | £160-240 | 6-8x |
| Outerwear (parka) | £50-80 | £280-480 | 5-7x |
| Tracksuit set | £35-50 | £200-340 | 5-7x |

The 5-10x ratio is the differentiation moat. Cannot be defended on logo alone — must be defended on weight, hand-feel, construction, story, scarcity.

## Operational metrics worth tracking
- **Time from concept to ad-live:** BoohooMAN: 36 hours. FTC target: 5-7 days for editorial, 24-48 hours for variant.
- **SKUs in catalog at any time:** BoohooMAN: 12,000+. FTC: 60-100 (live at any given moment).
- **Return rate:** BoohooMAN: ~30% (high). FTC target: <8%.
- **Customer lifetime value:** BoohooMAN: ~£90. FTC target: $800+.
- **Repeat purchase rate at 90 days:** BoohooMAN: ~40%. FTC target: 60%+.

## The mobile UX deep-dive (the only thing FTC borrows wholesale)

BoohooMAN app screens FTC should mirror in iPhone app structure:

1. **Home / featured drop:** large hero image, swipe-up = product grid.
2. **Product grid:** 2-column scroll, lazy-loaded images, filter bar pinned to top.
3. **Product detail:** swipeable image gallery, size + color selector pinned bottom, "Add to bag" CTA above all else.
4. **Bag:** stored payment + saved address; checkout in 2 taps.
5. **Profile:** order history, saved items, push-notification settings.

FTC additions on top of this base:
- **Drop countdown screen** with live timer.
- **Generative concept preview** — show the concept JSON-to-image generation in real time.
- **Story-first product view** — 90-second editorial vignette before the buy button.
- **Member tier display** — Bronze / Silver / Gold based on drop participation history.

## TL;DR — apply to FTC
1. **Match BoohooMAN's mobile UX speed, invert the product ethic.** Sub-second image loads, 2-tap checkout, infinite scroll on collection grid.
2. **48-hour generative ad variant cycle.** Operational speed at the creative pipeline (without sacrificing the artistic standard).
3. **Never crossed-out pricing. Never % off sales. Never urgency-fake-it copy.**
4. **5-10x BoohooMAN price defended by 5-10x quality:** weight, hand-feel, construction, story, scarcity.
5. **Customer service responsiveness matched, but with real humans on faith-aligned values.**
