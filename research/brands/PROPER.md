# PROPER — brand & magazine study

> "Proper Magazine" + "Proper Brand" — UK menswear publication and retailer based in Manchester. The most respected voice in adult menswear since 2008. Where serious heritage menswear (Engineered Garments, Beams, Stone Island, Tilak) meets serious editorial.

## Founding logic
- Premise: real menswear is for grown men with mortgages, not for 19-year-old hypebeasts.
- Aesthetic ancestor: The Face, Arena Homme+, GQ pre-2010.
- Customer: 28–55, UK + Western Europe, employed in design/architecture/media, $50k+ annual menswear spend over 10 years.

## Visual codes
| Element | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- |
| Color palette | Earth tones, navy, olive, brown, ecru. Almost zero black. No primaries ever. |
| Logos | Minimal. Tonal woven labels only. |
| Typography | A modern serif (cited as "Genath" or similar) for editorial; Helvetica Neue for navigation. |
| Silhouettes | Authentic heritage: BDU trousers, fishtail parkas, ranger jackets, fisherman knits, deck shoes. |
| Materials | Tilak Poutnik nylon, Beams's nep cotton, Engineered Garments's recycled wool, Drake's silk. |
| Footwear | Paraboot, Crockett & Jones, New Balance Made in UK, Hoka (for the dads-in-the-know move). |

## Strategic moves
- **Magazine + Shop = total ecosystem.** Editorial generates trust; shop converts it. No one buys from Proper because they saw an ad; they buy because they read 6 issues over 3 years and trust the curation.
- **Designer interviews, deep ones.** Not 800-word press-release rehashes. 4,000-word conversations with the maker of an obscure Czech military-cotton fabric. Customers feel they're being taught.
- **Curation as authority.** Proper carries 200 SKUs at any time, hand-selected. Compare to Mr Porter (10k+). Restraint reads as expertise.
- **Print magazine quarterly.** In a digital era, the printed magazine signals seriousness. Costs $80/year subscription; sells out.

## What we steal
1. **Magazine as primary marketing.** FTC's Substack should evolve into a quarterly print magazine. "FTC Quarterly" — 64 pages, no ads, real essays on faith and design. Year 3 goal.
2. **Curatorial restraint.** Even when FTC has 1000 designs (this season), the website should show 60–80 at any time. Surface "featured" via the iPhone app's first screen.
3. **Long-form designer interviews.** Founder + product team conversations published as 3000-word pieces twice per drop.
4. **Heritage menswear vocabulary.** Specific fabric names. Mill names. Construction technique names. "Garment-washed 320gsm Japanese loopback cotton from Loopwheeler" reads better than "premium quality."
5. **No ads.** FTC's editorial channels (Substack, podcast, YouTube) should never run third-party ads.

## What we avoid
1. **Demographic narrowness.** Proper skews 35+, very male, very UK/EU. FTC's audience is younger and more global. Aesthetic restraint, but accessibility.
2. **Heritage worship.** Proper sometimes reads as "anything old is good." FTC is making new things rooted in old faith, not curating old things.
3. **Manchester-specific cultural references.** Proper's voice is northern-English. FTC's voice should be diaspora-English (DMV/Toronto/Lagos/London) — different cadence.

## Materials lexicon to adopt
Proper's product pages teach the reader fabric vocabulary. FTC PDPs should match this depth:
- **GSM** (grams per square meter) — quantifies weight. State it. "320gsm cotton" not "heavyweight cotton."
- **Loopback / French terry / fleece / interlock** — specify the knit.
- **Garment-washed / piece-dyed / overdyed** — specify the dye process.
- **Mercerised / sanforised** — specify finishing.
- **Single-needle / chain-stitch / felled seam** — specify construction.
- **Made in Portugal / Made in Japan / Made in UK** — specify origin. Customers reward transparency.

## Photography & art direction
- Editorial-led, not product-led.
- Backdrops are real interiors: Manchester pubs, Northern Quarter coffee shops, brutalist car parks.
- Models are adults — 30s and 40s, often the designers themselves or longtime customers.
- Available light, slightly underexposed. No reflectors, no fill.
- Editing: very flat (D-Log or similar) with a 5% lift in mid-tones for that printed-on-uncoated-paper feel.

## Editorial voice — to study and adapt
Proper writing is:
- **Specific.** "The cotton comes from a mill in Mie Prefecture that opened in 1898" beats "premium quality."
- **Skeptical.** They'll say a product isn't worth the money. This builds trust.
- **British dry.** Not preachy. Not aspirational. Tone-down hard.
- **Image-rich.** Every paragraph has a photo. Words and images interleave.

FTC's editorial voice should:
- Be specific (✓ already aligned).
- Be capable of saying no (FTC review of a competitor's drop, honestly).
- Replace British dry with **monastic gravity** — same restraint, different cultural register.
- Image-interleave.

## The shop curation model
Proper's product page is its own design study:
1. **Hero image:** product on real backdrop (never cyclorama).
2. **Editorial paragraph:** 100–200 words on what this is, who made it, why it matters.
3. **Spec block:** material, country, dimensions, washing.
4. **Detail shots:** 4–8 close-ups of construction and detail.
5. **"How we wore it":** 1 styling shot from a real customer or staff member.

FTC product pages should match this 5-section structure exactly.

## Failure modes Proper has avoided (lessons)
- Never lowered prices to chase volume.
- Never expanded into footwear they couldn't curate at the same standard.
- Never accepted sponsored content. Editorial integrity is the entire moat.

## Failure modes to watch for
- **Insularity.** Northern England menswear culture is small. The same 15 brands appear in every issue. FTC must avoid this — keep finding new collaborators.
- **Too-male.** Proper's editorial almost ignores women. FTC has womenswear in roadmap; voice and imagery must equal-weight.

## TL;DR — apply to FTC
1. **Editorial-first marketing.** Substack → quarterly print magazine by year 3.
2. **Curatorial restraint** even at 1000 SKUs — surface 60–80 at a time.
3. **Long-form designer interviews** twice per drop.
4. **Materials lexicon** in every PDP — GSM, loopback, garment-washed, made-in-X.
5. **Editorial voice: specific, skeptical, monastic-grave, image-rich.**
6. **5-section product page structure** lifted directly from Proper.
